## Spacing issues found

### Critical / High impact
1. **ILn main.tex:22** — `\setlength{\parskip}{-4.5mm plus 0.5mm minus 0.5mm}`
   - Global negative `parskip` risks overlapping/compressing adjacent paragraphs.
   - Reads as a typo or unintended override (likely meant `0pt` or a small positive value).

### Medium impact
2. **main.tex:70** — `\vspace{-7ex}` after title
   - Aggressive upward pull; can cause the title/TOC to collide with the top margin or header rule.

3. **main.tex:33–34** — TOC entry hooks: `\renewcommand\cftsecafterpnum{\vspace{-0.5ex}}` and same for subsections
   - Shrinks spacing between section name and page number; can clip or visually crowd the TOC.

4. **Inconsistent local `{parskip=1pt` groups in section files**
   - `08_trie_and_booth.tex:80`, `09_basis_and_dp.tex:124`, `09_basis_and_dp.tex:298`, `09_basis_and_dp.tex:312`, `10_combinatorics.tex:53`, `11_geometry_and_misc.tex:3`
   - These are local group overrides that bypass the global `parskip`. Across sections this causes visual inconsistency (some text blocks look looser than others).

### Low impact
5. **main.tex:20** — `\columnseprule=0.1pt`
   - Very thin column rule; in 3-column landscape layout it may be visually indistinguishable.

6. **main.tex:42** — `\setminted` tight vertical spacing around minted blocks unless minted blocks’ top/footers are properly isolated. This is intentional for density, but double-check surrounding paragraph spacing.

## Proposed fixes

1. main.tex:22 — Set `\parskip` to `0pt plus 0pt minus 0pt` (or small positive/shrinking space appropriate for notebook layout, e.g., `0.5pt` with stretch/shrink).
2. main.tex:70 — Change `\vspace{-7ex}` to smaller value or remove.
3. main.tex:33–34 — Remove/reduce the `-0.5ex` TOC spacing hacks.
4. Replace the local `{parskip=1pt...}` blocks in section files with a consistent global spacing approach or remove them if global setting is tuned properly.
5. Optionally increase `\columnseprule` to `0.3pt–0.4pt` for better column readability.
6. Verify minted-to-subsection spacing remains acceptable after adjustment.
